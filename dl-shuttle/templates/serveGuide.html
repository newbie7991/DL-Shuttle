Backhand Short Serve


Backhand low serve is a basic serving shot that is widely use by many players. A good low serve

will have the shuttlecock flying just above the net and it prevents your opponent from making an offensive shot easily.

>







A Good Serve Trajectory




Tutorial


1. Stand 2 to 3 feet behind the short service line.

2. Relax your body and bent your knees slightly.

3. Lead with your non-racquet leg and place your racket leg behind.

4. Bring your racquet back to your waist level then start your forward swing.

5. Hold the shuttle by the feathers and bring ti closer to meet the racquet instead of dropping it in front.

6. Contact the shuttle at a higher point but !! Still below your waist line.

7. Push the shuttle with the racket face and try to make the shuttle skim the tape of the net.
