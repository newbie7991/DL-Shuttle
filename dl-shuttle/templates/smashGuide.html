Forehand Smash


A forehand smash is a cool and famous skill set that every badminton player looking
forward to learn. It is an offensive shot executed from the rear court that travels
downwards to your opponent's court.

>



Tutorial


1. Get into position and adopt the Forehand Grip. Ideally, your body should face the
side of the court. Both of your feet should point sideways.

2. Stand on a firm position. If you're not on good balance, your smash won't be strong
because your body will concentrate on balancing first rather than doing a strong swing

3. Raise your Racket Arm and Non-Racket Arm. Make sure you stretch your arm as far
as possible. This will help ensure you perform a full swing. At the same time, raise your
Non-Racquet Arm to balance out the weight of your Racket Arm.

4. Take the shuttle at the highest point possible to create a steep angle for your smash.

5. Hit the shuttle with a Full Arm Swing. Take a deep breath. Stretch out your chest to
the widest extend possible. Stretch out your Non-Racket Arm. Then swing your racquet
forward as you exhale. At the same time, contract your abs. Shuffle your racket Foot to
the front.

6. After you hit the shuttle, follow through and complete your swing. Your body should
face forward after you swing your racquet. Maintain body balance and recover.
