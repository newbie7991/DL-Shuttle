Forehand Clear Shot


A basic clear shot is a shot that is ideally played high, from the baseline
of one court to the baseline of the opponent's court. It is normally considered
as a defensive shot to give the player some recovery time and also to push the other
player towards the baseline.

>



Tutorial


1. Get ready for the shuttle to reach your court.

2. Move(not run) to the direction of the shuttle.

3. Take aim with your left hand. Your front foot(left) should point in
the direction in which you want to hit and your back foot should
approximately be at 90 degrees to it.

4. Raise your racquet over your head, rotate using your arm and slap
the shuttle upwards using your wrist.

5. While you are ready to hit, all your weight should be on your back
foot. This will help you use your body force for the stroke.
6. Contact with the shuttle should be make when you are directly
below the shuttle and at the highest point.

7. After hitting the shuttle your racquet should come down to your
left side in a swinging motion.

8. Simultaneosly, your back foot should come in the front, completing
the action and thus helping you move to the middle of the court again.
